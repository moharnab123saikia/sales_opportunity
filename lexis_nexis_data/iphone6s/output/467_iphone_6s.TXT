Advance Booking Starts for iPhone 6s and 6s Plus



BYLINE: Gulveen.Aulakh@timesgroup.com



SECTION: COMPANIES



LENGTH: 330 words





Retailers have started taking bookings for Apple's latest iPhones and those

waiting to get their hands on them should be prepared to shell out ' . 62,000

for the lowest storage variant, 16 GB, of the iPhone 6s and as much as . 92,000

for the 128 GB variant of ' the iPhone 6s Plus.



According to three trade sources, Apple has communicated the indicative prices

and given the goahead to begin taking advance orders. Industry officials told ET

the iPhone 6s 64 GB would cost about '. 72,000 and the 128 GB version would be

about ' . 82,000. The iPhone 6s Plus 16 GB would be priced at ' . 72,000 and its

larger storage model of 64 GB would be priced at '. 82,000.



Apple is yet to declare official prices in India, the company said in response

to an emailed query.



Pre-bookings have started at major retail chains across the country. The

MobileStore, India's largest mobile phone retail chain, said it will begin

taking orders from Friday. The Mumbai-based chain with over 800 outlets will

take pre-orders for ' . 2,000 each up



to October 13. The devices will become available on the night of October 15.



"The iPhone 6s 64 GB variant is the most popular one as of now," said Satish

Babu, founder of Chennai-based UniverCell Mobiles, one of the largest retail

chains in south India, which has started taking pre-orders across its 300

stores. "Since yesterday we



have got at least 100 or ders," he added. Bengaluru-based Sangeetha Mo biles' MD

Subhash Chandra said the company had started taking pre orders for ' . 1,000

apiece t and expects most existing iPhone users to upgrade to the new models

within the first month.



The retail chains will offer EMI payment options, up grade programmes and fil

nance schemes. "We will al so offer freebies like back case and accessories

during the booking period," Satish Babu added. Sources add Apple is set to get

on a , marketing blitzkrieg and retailers are expecting much higher sales than

last l year as Apple plans to make adequate stocks available.
