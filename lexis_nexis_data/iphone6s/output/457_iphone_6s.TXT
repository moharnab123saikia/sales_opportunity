Chennai, Oct. 10 -- If you have bought the new "iPhone 6S or iPhone 6S Plus",
then its battery benchmark must be checked, as it comes with two different
standards.

After the difference in the battery standards of both the assemblies was noted,
the iPhone 6S and 6S Plus has again captured the news.

Apple has used A9 chips manufactured by two different companies for assembling
the same model of iPhone handsets.

Reportedly, the manufacturing of assembled A9 chips was outsourced to TSMC and
Samsung, but only after a month of the launch of iPhone 6S, TSMC's chip over
powered the battery standards of one's manufactured by Samsung.

The only drawback with the Samsung's assembly is that the phone's battery drains
much faster than TSMC's.

Regardless of the variation in the battery benchmarks of both the assemblies,
the features of the phone are not affected.
